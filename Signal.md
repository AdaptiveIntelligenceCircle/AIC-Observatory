# Signal Sources

## Overview

AIC Observatory relies on a diverse set of signal sources to monitor the evolution of technology, information ecosystems, economic systems, and societal dynamics.

Signals are collected from publicly available and aggregated data sources.

The project explicitly avoids:

* personal data collection
* individual tracking
* surveillance systems

All signals must operate at the **system-level**.

---

# Signal Categories

Signals are grouped into several primary categories.

```id="sigflow"
Signal Sources
│
├── Technology Signals
├── Research Signals
├── Economic Signals
├── Information Ecosystem Signals
└── Societal Signals
```

Each category captures different aspects of civilizational change.

---

# Technology Signals

Technology signals monitor the evolution of global technological infrastructure.

### Open Source Ecosystems

Possible sources:

* GitHub repository activity
* open-source contribution trends
* project sustainability metrics
* contributor diversity

Signals may include:

* repository growth rate
* number of active contributors
* project dependency networks

---

### AI Development

Signals related to artificial intelligence progress may include:

* model capability benchmarks
* open-source model releases
* AI infrastructure investments
* AI research activity

---

### Compute Infrastructure

Signals tracking compute resources may include:

* GPU market distribution
* cloud infrastructure expansion
* hyperscale data center development
* semiconductor production trends

---

# Research Signals

Research ecosystems often produce early indicators of technological change.

Possible signals include:

* research publication volume
* citation trends
* emerging research topics
* interdisciplinary research activity

Sources may include:

* academic repositories
* open-access journals
* research collaboration networks

---

# Economic Signals

Economic signals capture the impact of technology on economic systems.

Possible signals include:

* investment in AI and automation
* startup formation rates
* venture capital activity
* labor market shifts related to automation

---

# Information Ecosystem Signals

Information environments strongly influence societal cognition.

Signals may include:

* information diversity metrics
* misinformation propagation patterns
* social media discourse dynamics
* digital attention trends

Signals must be aggregated and anonymized.

---

# Societal Signals

Societal signals attempt to measure large-scale social and cultural dynamics.

Possible signals include:

* indicators of social participation
* measures of digital engagement
* indicators of community activity
* global knowledge production trends

---

# Signal Quality Requirements

All signals must meet several criteria:

### Public Availability

Signals must come from publicly accessible or aggregated sources.

### Aggregation

Signals must represent system-level patterns.

### Transparency

Signal origins and processing methods must be documented.

### Reliability

Signals must undergo validation before entering analytical pipelines.

---

# Signal Processing Pipeline

Signals follow a structured pipeline before being used by Observatory models.

```id="signalpipe"
Signal Collection
      ↓
Signal Validation
      ↓
Signal Normalization
      ↓
Pattern Detection
      ↓
Civilization Modeling
```

The Observatory may incorporate additional signal sources as the project evolves.

# Ethical Considerations

Signal collection must always respect:

* privacy
* transparency
* ethical research standards
