# Ethics Framework

## Purpose

AIC Observatory analyzes signals related to technological development and societal dynamics.

Because these systems operate at the scale of human civilization, ethical safeguards are essential.

This document outlines the ethical principles guiding the project.

---

# Core Ethical Principles

### Human Dignity

All technologies developed within this project must respect human dignity and autonomy.

### Privacy Protection

The system must never track individuals or collect personally identifiable data.

Signals must remain system-level and aggregated.

### Non-Manipulation

The project must not contribute to technologies designed to manipulate public opinion, cognition, or behavior.

### Responsible Foresight

Simulation tools should be used to understand potential risks and opportunities, not to predict or control human behavior.

### Open Knowledge

Insights should be shared openly when possible to support responsible decision-making.

---

# Ethical Risk Areas

The following areas require special caution.

### Cognitive Influence Analysis

Models analyzing information dynamics must avoid enabling propaganda or manipulation.

### Social Signal Interpretation

Researchers should avoid drawing deterministic conclusions about complex human behaviors.

### Technology Risk Forecasting

Forecasts must include uncertainty and avoid presenting predictions as certainty.

---

# Ethical Review

Certain contributions may require ethical review by project maintainers, particularly if they involve:

* new signal sources
* social data analysis
* cognitive modeling techniques

---

# Misuse Prevention

AIC Observatory should not be used for:

* mass surveillance
* social manipulation
* targeted behavioral prediction
* political propaganda

Contributors must actively prevent such misuse.

---

# Ethical Responsibility of Contributors

Contributors are expected to:

* respect the project's ethical principles
* document limitations and uncertainties
* avoid misuse of research outputs
