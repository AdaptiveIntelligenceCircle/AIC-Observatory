# Governance Model

## Overview

AIC Observatory is an open research infrastructure developed under the Adaptive Intelligence Circle (AIC).

The governance model is designed to ensure:

* transparency
* ethical responsibility
* scientific integrity
* long-term sustainability

The project aims to serve humanity by providing insight into large-scale civilizational dynamics.

---

# Governance Principles

The governance of AIC Observatory follows five core principles.

### 1. Transparency

All major decisions regarding architecture, models, and policies should be publicly documented whenever possible.

### 2. Ethical Responsibility

The project must avoid contributing to systems that enable surveillance, manipulation, or harm to individuals or societies.

### 3. Scientific Integrity

All research outputs should maintain high standards of academic and engineering rigor.

### 4. Privacy Protection

The project does not collect or process personal data.

All signals should be aggregated, anonymized, or derived from public sources.

### 5. Long-Term Civilization Perspective

Decisions should consider long-term impacts on human civilization rather than short-term incentives.

---

# Governance Structure

The project is managed through the following roles.

### Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for:

* reviewing pull requests
* approving architectural changes
* managing releases
* enforcing governance policies

### Research Contributors

Researchers contribute models, analyses, and simulations.

Their responsibilities include:

* documenting research assumptions
* providing reproducible experiments
* respecting ethical guidelines

### Community Contributors

Community members may contribute:

* code
* documentation
* signal sources
* analysis tools

---

# Decision-Making Process

Significant decisions follow this process:

1. proposal submitted
2. public discussion
3. review by maintainers
4. final decision and documentation

Large architectural changes may require a formal proposal document.

---

# Ethical Oversight

Certain contributions may require ethical review, especially when related to:

* social signal analysis
* cognitive influence modeling
* systemic risk simulations

Ethical review ensures that the project remains aligned with its founding principles.

---

# Governance Evolution

The governance model may evolve as the project grows.

Any governance changes must be publicly documented and discussed with the community.
