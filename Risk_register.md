# Risk Register

## Purpose

The AIC Observatory analyzes signals and trends affecting human civilization.

Because the project operates at a systemic level, it must actively track and manage risks related to:

* analytical reliability
* misuse of insights
* technological bias
* governance failures

This document provides a structured overview of major risks and mitigation strategies.

---

# Risk Categories

## Analytical Risks

### Model Misinterpretation

Civilization models may produce probabilistic insights that could be interpreted as deterministic predictions.

Risk:

Decision-makers may treat projections as guaranteed outcomes.

Mitigation:

* probabilistic outputs
* uncertainty reporting
* scenario diversity

---

### Signal Noise

Civilizational signals may contain significant noise due to incomplete or biased data sources.

Risk:

False trends or misleading signals.

Mitigation:

* cross-signal validation
* multi-source aggregation
* anomaly detection

---

## Data Risks

### Data Bias

Global signals may overrepresent certain regions or communities.

Risk:

Incomplete representation of global civilization dynamics.

Mitigation:

* geographically diverse data sources
* signal diversity requirements
* transparency about data limitations

---

### Signal Manipulation

External actors may attempt to manipulate observable signals.

Examples:

* coordinated misinformation campaigns
* bot-generated information trends
* artificial research amplification

Mitigation:

* anomaly detection
* trust scoring systems
* signal integrity monitoring

---

## Technological Risks

### Model Overconfidence

AI-based models may present insights with insufficient uncertainty quantification.

Risk:

False sense of predictive accuracy.

Mitigation:

* explicit uncertainty ranges
* model evaluation pipelines
* scenario-based analysis

---

### Infrastructure Vulnerabilities

Signal ingestion or simulation pipelines may contain technical vulnerabilities.

Risk:

data corruption or analytical failure.

Mitigation:

* secure infrastructure
* input validation
* reproducible analysis pipelines

---

## Governance Risks

### Centralization of Authority

If governance becomes concentrated, the project could lose credibility.

Mitigation:

* transparent governance processes
* community oversight
* open decision documentation

---

### Strategic Misuse

Civilization-scale insights could theoretically be used by actors seeking to exploit systemic vulnerabilities.

Mitigation:

* responsible disclosure policies
* ethical review of sensitive outputs
* selective publication of high-risk insights

---

# Risk Monitoring

Risks will be periodically reviewed and updated.

The risk register should evolve alongside the project's architecture and research scope.
