# Civilization Indicators

## Overview

AIC Observatory tracks a set of indicators designed to measure large-scale civilizational dynamics.

These indicators help identify long-term trends related to:

* technological development
* societal stability
* cognitive environments
* systemic risks

Indicators are designed to operate at **system-level**, not individual-level.

---

# Technology Indicators

### AI Acceleration Index

Measures the rate of progress in artificial intelligence.

Factors may include:

* model capability improvements
* AI research output
* AI infrastructure expansion

---

### Compute Concentration Index

Tracks the degree to which compute resources are centralized among a small number of organizations.

High concentration may increase systemic risk.

---

### Open Source AI Health Index

Measures the vitality of open-source AI ecosystems.

Factors:

* repository activity
* contributor diversity
* community engagement

---

# Societal Indicators

### Meaning Stability Index

Measures the stability of meaning structures within society.

Signals may include:

* loneliness indicators
* community engagement metrics
* long-term social participation trends

---

### Attention Fragmentation Index

Measures how fragmented public attention becomes within digital environments.

Signals may include:

* short-form content dominance
* attention half-life of information
* engagement volatility

---

### Cognitive Manipulation Risk Index

Tracks signals related to manipulation of public cognition.

Examples:

* misinformation amplification
* algorithmic outrage dynamics
* AI-generated content proliferation

---

# Systemic Risk Indicators

### Technology Disruption Risk

Measures the probability that technological change may destabilize economic or political systems.

---

### Information Ecosystem Stability

Evaluates the health and resilience of information environments.

Signals may include:

* information diversity
* media trust
* misinformation spread

---

# Indicator Design Principles

Indicators must follow several principles:

1. system-level analysis
2. transparency of methodology
3. reproducibility
4. uncertainty reporting

Indicators should evolve as new signals and models become available.
